Northern Virginia native Chris D'Agostino has been a technology leader in the Intelligence Community for the past decade. Since founding Reston-based software development firm Near Infinity Corporation in 2002, he has been involved with solving some of the government's most pressing IT challenges, including making significant contributions to mobile development and big data search.

As Founder of Near Infinity, D'Agostino focused on building a company for developers, a company that would attract extraordinary software developers who loved to learn and who would stay on the cutting edge of evolving technology and development process. Through award-winning employee development and training programs, as well as a transparent, employee-centric culture and benefits program, D'Agostino has grown the company to over $11M in revenue with an average 90% annual retention rate.

In 2010, Near Infinity was named one of the Top 25 Best Small-Sized Companies to Work for in America by entrepreneur.com's Great Place to Work Institute. The company has been listed two consecutive years on the Inc 500 | 5000 List and has three times won the Alliance for Workplace Excellence's award for outstanding commitment to overall workplace quality. It was also named to the Virginia Chamber of Commerce's Fantastic 50 list, which recognizes Virginia's 50 fastest growing companies each year.

In addition to the past ten years with Near Infinity, D'Agostino has worked in Northern Virginia since 1989, focusing his career on software development and engineering for the Intelligence Community.

D'Agostino is an adjunct professor at George Mason University's Volgenau School of Engineering, as well as the University of Virginia. He is also a member of the Virginia Tech Intelligence Defense Executive Alumni group (VT-IDEA), an organization working to prepare the region to best serve the Intelligence Community in the future. He holds an B.S. and M.S. in Electrical Engineering from Virginia Tech.
